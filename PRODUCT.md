# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Sechat is for broad, everyday chat use. Anyone should be able to enter a room, talk about anything they want, share media, reply, mention people, and use voice without the interface making the moment feel heavy or formal.

## Product Purpose

Sechat provides a minimal realtime chat room with Firebase-backed sync, local fallback storage, attachments, replies, mentions, notifications, voice messages, voice chat, moderation, and profile customization. Success means the app feels immediate, expressive, and dependable while staying compact enough for repeated daily use on desktop and mobile.

## Brand Personality

Compact, tactile, lively, animated. The app should feel responsive in the hand: dark, direct, playful in motion, and personal through customization without becoming noisy or decorative.

## Anti-references

Do not make Sechat feel like a generic dashboard, a bright settings page, a marketing site, or a heavy enterprise chat client. Avoid large explanatory cards, colorful decoration, scattered bright CTAs, native browser tooltips, new icon/UI stacks, and any one-off visual language that breaks from the composer and top-chrome family.

## Design Principles

- Keep the chat first: every feature should preserve fast reading, writing, replying, and voice participation.
- Make customization feel native: profile, notification, sound, media, and room controls should be discoverable without turning into bulky settings pages.
- Stay compact but alive: use dense layouts, short labels, tactile controls, and purposeful motion for state and feedback.
- Extend the composer language: new controls should inherit the dark matte surfaces, pill actions, subtle borders, and bottom-safe rhythm already in the app.
- Build for everyone: broad-use chat needs strong mobile reachability, keyboard and screen reader support, reduced-motion paths, and clear states for loading, errors, permissions, and disabled actions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Continue supporting reduced motion, mobile reachability, visible focus states, keyboard navigation, screen reader labels, and non-native tooltips. New features should preserve or improve those affordances, especially around floating panels, composer-adjacent controls, media/GIF UI, voice controls, and customizable notification/profile settings.
